Creative is where Emma's generated assets land. Five sub-sections, each one a different output type.

| Sub-section | What's there |
|---|---|
| [Social Posts](#social-posts) | Cross-platform social drafts, scheduled posts, post performance. |
| [LinkedIn Content](#linkedin-content) | LinkedIn-specific posts and thought leadership drafts. |
| [Images](#images) | AI-generated visuals — hero images, post graphics, ad creatives. |
| [Videos](#videos) | AI-generated short-form video for social and ads. |
| [Landing Pages](#landing-pages) | Conversion-optimized landing pages for campaigns and lead capture. |

You can generate any of these from a conversation in [Agent Mode](/user-guide/agent-mode) — output lands in the relevant Creative sub-section automatically.

## Social Posts

Cross-platform social content: drafts, scheduled posts, and per-post performance.

**How to use:**

1. Ask Emma to draft posts in [Agent Mode](/user-guide/agent-mode). Specify the platform, audience, and angle.
2. Review the drafts in **Creative → Social Posts**. Edit copy, swap images.
3. Schedule directly from the post detail view, or send to your connected account immediately.
4. Once published, performance metrics flow back into the post view.

**Watch-outs:**

- Different platforms reward different formats. Don't cross-post a 280-character X post to LinkedIn unedited.
- Schedule based on when your audience is online, not when you happen to be drafting.

## LinkedIn Content

LinkedIn-specific drafts. Optimized for thought leadership and engagement formats — long-form personal essays, founder narratives, hot takes.

**How to use:**

1. Ask Emma to draft a LinkedIn post in [Agent Mode](/user-guide/agent-mode). Mention the founder voice and the angle you want.
2. Review the drafts in **Creative → LinkedIn Content**.
3. Schedule or publish directly to your connected LinkedIn profile or company page.

LinkedIn drafts use your founder profile from [Brand → Brand Guidelines](/user-guide/brand#brand-guidelines). If posts don't sound like you, the founder voice description there is the place to fix it.

## Images

AI-generated visuals for ads, blogs, landing pages, and social.

**Common outputs:**

- Ad creatives
- Blog hero images
- Social visuals
- Branded illustrations

**How to use:**

1. Generate images from a text prompt — either in Agent Mode or from the Images page.
2. Edit images inline — add logos, adjust layouts, change styles.
3. Generate variations for testing.
4. Download for use outside the portal, or attach directly to a social post or landing page.

**Watch-outs:**

- Always specify usage context (ad, blog hero, square social, story). Emma uses this to size and frame correctly.
- Reference your brand colors and logo from Brand for consistency across variations.
- Test in context. An image that looks good in the editor can look wrong in the actual placement.

## Videos

Generate short-form video and analyze video performance.

**What's in this section:**

- Video generation from prompts
- Performance analysis on videos you've published
- Competitor video breakdowns

**How to use:**

1. Generate video concepts from a text prompt.
2. Analyze existing videos for messaging, pacing, and hooks.
3. Apply insights to improve your own creative.
4. Export for ads or social platforms.

**Watch-outs:**

- The first 3 seconds matter most. If you're not stopping the scroll there, the rest doesn't matter.
- Match length and format to platform norms. A 60-second YouTube clip and a 15-second Reel are different products.
- Optimize for clarity, not complexity.

## Landing Pages

Conversion-optimized landing pages for campaigns and lead capture.

**How to use:**

1. Ask Emma to generate a landing page in [Agent Mode](/user-guide/agent-mode). Provide the campaign objective, audience, offer, and primary CTA.
2. Review the generated page in **Creative → Landing Pages**. Edit copy and sections inline.
3. Deploy the page to a subdomain. Emma sets up the routing.
4. Track conversion performance from the same view.

**Watch-outs:**

- One CTA per page. Two CTAs means half conversion on both.
- The headline does most of the work. Iterate the headline before you iterate anything else on the page.
- Mobile preview before publishing. Most paid traffic is mobile.
