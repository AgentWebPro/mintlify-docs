Google Ads connects through a self-serve OAuth flow in **Settings → Accounts**. Once a standard Google Ads account is connected, Emma can create budgets and campaigns, manage ad groups, keywords, and ads, and pull performance reports.

Google Ads is available on the **Pro** plan. If you don't see the Google Ads card in **Settings → Accounts**, your plan doesn't include it yet. Contact [support@agentweb.pro](mailto:support@agentweb.pro) to upgrade.

## Before you begin

You connect by signing in with a Google account. For an ad account to appear in the connect flow, that Google account must have **direct access to a standard Google Ads account**. This is the single most common reason a connection succeeds but no account shows up, so confirm it first.

Work through the checks below in the [Google Ads UI](https://ads.google.com) before connecting.

<Steps>
  <Step title="You have a standard Google Ads account, not just a manager account">
    A manager account (also called an MCC, "My Client Center") is a container for managing other accounts. It can't run ads itself, and AgentWeb intentionally hides manager accounts from the connect list. You need at least one standard account.

    To check: open [ads.google.com](https://ads.google.com) and look at the account switcher in the top bar. If every entry is labeled **Manager**, you don't have a standard account yet. Create one from the manager account under **Accounts → Sub-account settings → Create new account**, or start a new standard account directly at [ads.google.com](https://ads.google.com).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your Google login has direct access to that account">
    `listAccessibleCustomers`, the Google Ads endpoint AgentWeb uses, only returns accounts your Google login is linked to **directly**. Access inherited through a manager hierarchy does not count. If you manage the account through an MCC, add your Google login as a user on the account itself.

    In Google Ads: **Admin → Access and security → Users → +** , add your email, and accept the invite. Standard or Admin access works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The account is set up and active">
    Brand-new accounts that never finished setup (no billing, no campaign) can fail to return account details and get skipped. Open the account once in the Google Ads UI and complete the initial setup, including a billing/payment method, before connecting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You know which Google account holds the ad account">
    If you have several Google logins, note which one the ad account lives under. You'll sign in with exactly that account during the connect flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  AgentWeb shows only **standard** Google Ads accounts. Manager (MCC) accounts are filtered out by design, the same way the Meta Ads integration handles Meta Business Manager accounts.
</Note>

## Connect

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Accounts">
    In the Customer Portal, go to **Settings → Accounts** and find the Google Ads card. (Google Ads can't be connected during onboarding. Use Settings.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect">
    AgentWeb opens Google's OAuth consent screen in a popup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in to Google">
    Sign in with the Google account that has direct access to the ad account you want to manage. Grant the requested Google Ads permission when prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the ad account">
    AgentWeb lists every standard account your Google login can access. Each row shows the account name, the numeric customer ID, and the account currency. Select the one to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Connect**. The card flips to **Connected** and shows the account you picked. You can connect more than one Google Ads account by repeating the flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Emma can do once connected

- List campaigns, ad groups, ads, keywords, and budgets in the connected account.
- Create campaign budgets and launch Search, Display, and Performance Max campaigns from briefs you describe in [Agent Mode](/user-guide/agent-mode).
- Add and update keywords, including negative keywords, and set bids and bidding strategies.
- Pause, resume, update, and remove campaigns, ad groups, and ads.
- Upload image, logo, and video assets and attach them to campaigns.
- Pull performance reports (spend, impressions, clicks, CTR, conversions, average CPC) for any date range, either as a formatted summary or a custom query.

Campaign metrics surface in [Performance](/user-guide/performance). Spend is reported in the account's own currency.

## Disconnect

Click the Google Ads card in **Settings → Accounts** and choose **Disconnect**. To revoke AgentWeb's access on the Google side, go to your [Google Account → Security → Third-party access](https://myaccount.google.com/connections) and remove AgentWeb. Disconnecting stops Emma from acting on the account. It doesn't change anything inside Google Ads.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Connection succeeds but no ad account appears">
    This almost always means the Google login you used has no **standard** Google Ads account with **direct** access. Check, in order:

    1. The Google account you signed in with is the one that owns the ad account. Sign out and retry with the correct account if not.
    2. You have a standard account, not only a manager (MCC) account. Manager accounts are hidden.
    3. Your login is added directly as a user on the ad account under **Admin → Access and security → Users**, not only through a manager hierarchy.
    4. The account finished setup and has a billing method. Brand-new, unconfigured accounts can be skipped.

    Fix the gap in the Google Ads UI, then click **Connect** again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Only manager accounts show in Google Ads but nothing connects">
    A manager (MCC) account can't run ads and is filtered out. Create a standard sub-account under the manager (**Accounts → Sub-account settings → Create new account**), add your Google login to it directly, then reconnect.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The connect popup expires before I finish">
    The account-selection step must be completed within about 10 minutes of signing in. If you wait too long, you'll see a session-expired message. Click **Connect** again to restart the flow.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reports or campaign actions fail after connecting">
    Open the account in the Google Ads UI and confirm it's active with a valid billing method and isn't suspended. Resolve any account-level alerts, then retry. If the failure persists, disconnect and reconnect to refresh access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The Google Ads card isn't in Settings → Accounts">
    Google Ads is a Pro-plan integration. If the card is missing, your current plan doesn't include it. Contact [support@agentweb.pro](mailto:support@agentweb.pro).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
